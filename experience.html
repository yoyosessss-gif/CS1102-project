A Practical Guide to Building Legal LLM Tools

Step 1: Data Preparation

    Remove Sensitive Information: Before you upload any documents, you must remove names, phone numbers, or secret client details. This protects the firm if there is a data leak.

    Build a Clean Library: Put the best templates, past opinions, and court rules of the firm into one secure digital folder.

    Make Files Searchable: Use OCR software to turn scanned PDFs into searchable text, AI cannot read a document if it is just image.

Step 2: Choose How to Build It

There is two ways of coding for users to choose

    The No Code method: Use platforms like Microsoft Copilot Studio. You can build AI agents by clicking buttons and typing instructions in English.

    The Low Code method: Use frameworks like Python. This method is more complex but it allows the AI to plan and finish many steps at once, such as reading many contracts at once and writing a summary for each.

Step 3: Use Open Book Reasoning (RAG)

AI often makes up fake information. To prevent this, you must use Retrieval Augmented Generation

    How RAG Works: Instead of guessing from memory, the AI must look for the answer inside your uploaded documents first.

    Set Strict Rules: Restrict the AI to say it does not know the answer if the information is not in its files. This prevents the AI from making things up.

Step 4: Master Legal Prompt Engineering

Step 4: Master Legal Prompt Engineering

he quality of the answer from the AI depends on your instructions and prompt. A professional prompt should include 4 things.

  1. Role: Tell the AI to act as a senior lawyer or a specialist.

    2. EG. "You are a senior IP lawyer with 20 years of experience."

  2. Task: Give a specific job, such as checking a contract for any missing rules about ending the agreement.

    3. EG. "Review this document and list any clauses that are unfair to our client."

  3. Format: Tell the AI how to show and display the work.

    4. EG. "Put the results in a bulleted list with a summary paragraph at the end."

  4. Citations: Ask the AI to list the exact page number and paragraph for every claim it makes.

    5. EG. "Tell me exactly which page and paragraph you found it on."

Step 5: Testing and Human Oversight

AI should act as an assisting tool, however the lawyer should double check the info.

    Human Verification: AI is only a helper for the office. A qualified lawyer must review output that the AI creates before it is finalized.

    The Accuracy Test: Create test questions where you know the answer. If the AI fails to answer the question, you should fix your instructions or data.
