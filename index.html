Background (The "Why")

The legal industry is shifting from basic keyword searches to AI-driven cognitive automation. Traditionally, legal tech relied on manual reviews and simple databases, which struggled to handle the massive volume of legal data.

Today, AI has evolved to comprehend legal context and logic; for instance, LLMs like GPT-4 can now pass bar exams. This demonstrates that AI can replace outdated manual processes that are no longer economically viable or efficient.

2. Key Functions

  • Automated Summarization: Condensing transcripts into executive summaries.
  • Semantic Retrieval: Finding precedents by describing a "scenario".
  • Automated Drafting: Generating first drafts of standard agreements.

3. Application Scenarios

  • Law Firms: Speeding up Discovery and Due Diligence.
  • In-house Legal Depts: Streamlining contract lifecycle management.
  • Judicial Sector: Enhancing access to justice.
