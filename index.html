1. Background

The legal industry is shifting from basic keyword searches to AI-driven cognitive automation. Traditionally, legal tech relied on manual reviews and simple databases, which is hard for people to handle the massive volume of legal data. It also creates challenges for people for understand the deeper meaning of laws. Since 80% of legal work involves messy, unstructured documents, the old way of having lawyers read everything is too slow and expensive.

Today, AI has evolved to understand legal context and logic. For exmaple, LLMs like GPT-4 can pass bar exams, showing they are no longer just search tools. They understand complex rules and context rather than just matching words. This shift allows AI to replace outdated manual processes that are no longer cost effective or efficient.

2. Key Functions

1. Smart Summaries

Legal documents often consist for hundreds or even thousands of pages. LLMs can accurately summarize the key points and generate a clear summary quickly. For example, in personal injury cases, AI can automatically process massive medical records. This helps lawyers quickly understand the extent of the injuries and what exactly caused them.

2. Better Searching

Instead of just looking for matching words, LLMs use "Semantic Search." Users can ask questions in simple words like: "What kind of money can I get for a personal injury?" and the AI can understands what the user mean. It finds the most relevant laws and past cases based on the user's question, not just the specific words you typed. Moreoever, some more advanced AI can scan through over 300 court judgments at once. It automatically picks out the main legal issues, this saves time of lawyers from reading every single page themselves.

3. Writing Documents

AI can write contract drafts automatically. By using professional templates and specific details—like the transaction price or ownership percentages—the AI builds the document for you [12, 13]. When combined with contract management systems, the AI can handle the entire lifecycle, from the first draft and negotiation all the way to the final signing [13].

4. Spotting Risks and Compliance

LLMs are also great at acting as a "safety check." You can give the AI specific rules to follow—for example, "Penalties cannot be more than 20% of the total price." If a contract breaks that rule, the AI will flag the risk and suggest a better way to word the clause so it follows the law.

3. Application Scenarios

  • Law Firms: Speeding up Discovery and Due Diligence.
  • In-house Legal Depts: Streamlining contract lifecycle management.
  • Judicial Sector: Enhancing access to justice.
